# Advanced Forensic Intelligence Report  
**Deep Packet Analysis**

---

## Vulnerabilities Identified  

1. **Mixed Traffic Vulnerability**: The capture shows a significant overlap between TCP and UDP traffic (TCP: 73%, UDP: 76%). This mixed traffic can be exploited for potential malicious intent, as the network is transmitting both structured (TCP) and unstructured (UDP) packets without specific knowledge of their origin.

2. **Low distinguishable host IPs**: With only 9 out of 207 hosts showing distinct packet signatures, it suggests that these devices may have underlying vulnerabilities or are targeting specific, unknown entities.

---

## Threat Classification  

1. **Threat Type: High TCP Traffic**  
   - The overwhelming majority (73% of captures) fall under TCP traffic classification. This could indicate a classified threat, as TCP is more structured and harder to track without prior knowledge of the network's behavior or specific threats.

2. **Vulnerability in UDP**: Given the low number of distinguishable host IPs, TCP traffic alone may not adequately cover all potential threats. However, UDP traffic (58% of captures) could be used to detect unknown devices or unusual patterns if specific packet analysis methods are applied.

---

## Countermeasures  

1. **Enhanced TCP Monitoring and Filtering**:  
   - Implement robust encryption for network traffic to prevent unauthorized access.  
   - Use traditional traffic analysis techniques without relying on IP addresses unless necessary (e.g., Nmap, OWASP APTIC tools).  
   - Filter out known malicious IPs in the future if they are identified.

2. **Improved UDP Monitoring**:  
   - Limit the use of packet filtering on UDP traffic to reduce detection risk.  
   - Implement network traffic analysis techniques without relying heavily on IP addresses (e.g., statistical outlier detection methods).

3. **Increased Statistical Monitoring for Outliers**:  
   - Continuously monitor for anomalies in packet patterns that may indicate a security breach or unexpected activity.

---

## Conclusion  

The mixed TCP-UDP traffic and low number of distinguishable host IPs suggest significant vulnerabilities and potential threats. By adopting enhanced TCP monitoring, improved UDP analysis, and increased statistical outlier detection, these vulnerabilities can be mitigated to protect network integrity and enhance security posture.
